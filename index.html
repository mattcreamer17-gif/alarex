The frame

Most of what ALAREX is building is not AI work.

BeachLens is live, two thousand users in the first month, and a Waze-style reporting layer on top of real-time Florida water data. EDEN AI and Trident are in alpha with a call out for fifty pilots. Aerobian is in the garage.

The beachgoer app is traditional code and database work with a live-data feed layered on top. The business dashboard is a two-sided marketplace motion that lives and dies on sales cadence, not on model quality. EDEN AI is the one piece where the model itself is the product, and even there, the hardest problems sit below the model: sensor data pipeline, label quality, accuracy documentation that a county or state procurement team can sign off on.

Our working split on a stack like this is sixty percent traditional code and database work, thirty percent rule-based logic (water-quality thresholds, alert rules, dashboard triggers), ten percent genuine AI. Putting an LLM where Postgres would do the job faster and cheaper is the cost most founders pay. We would rather help you see it early.

The three tracks

One founder, three surfaces, one engagement window.

Track 01 · Consumer

BeachLens

Real-time beach conditions, Waze-style reporting, personalized picks. Live on the App Store. The near-term revenue story is the beachfront-business dashboard and how fast app density converts to paid subscribers.

Track 02 · Environmental AI

EDEN AI

Harmful-algae-bloom and water-quality detection. The model is the product here. The procurement gate is a model card, an accuracy methodology, and label-quality documentation a county or state office can sign off on.

Track 03 · Hardware

Trident

Surface vessel for water-quality monitoring and response. Once fifty pilots are reporting, the live question is where that data lives and who owns the pipeline from sensor to dashboard.

Precedent

The shape of the engagement that would actually help.

Case · Feeld

A scoped sprint with a product company's CTO.

My colleague Jake ran a scoped sprint with Andrew Santus, CTO of Feeld. A product company with multiple AI surfaces, a founder trying to figure out what to build in-house and what to partner out. Deliverables were a workshop, advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework. That is the shape of the engagement that would actually help here.

Method · ICM

Interpretable Context Methodology.

Jake's methodology is published in ACM TiiS. It treats agent and project context as a layered file structure, which is useful when you are running three product lines with a student team. Repo: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.
